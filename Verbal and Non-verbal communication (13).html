Verbal and non-verbal communication

There are two major types of communication: verbal and non-verbal communication. Verbal communication 15 the type of communication in which words are used either in oral or written form whereas non-verbal communication is the type of communication in which words are not used. There are five components in non-verbal communication which are given below:

1. Kinesis:

  • In kinesis, the movement of our body conveys many different and specific meanings whose interpretation may be culture-bound. Since many of these movements occur unconsciously or subconsciously, there is a significantly larger risk of misinterpretation.
  • Kinesis includes facial expressions, hand movement, gestures, body language, etc.
  • Direct eye contact with the audience is essential for every speaker. A major barrier to face to face communication is the avoidance of eye contact. Similarly, body language and posture reflect greatly on spoken words. A straight and erect back conveys that person is confident while speaking, while a hunched back reflects poor confidence level, especially during interviews.

    • 2. Proxemics:

  • proxemics means distance. The measure of distance we keep from others, conveys the relationship we share with them or the subject we are discussing.

  • Intimate distance: contact to 18 inches
  • Personal distance: 18 inches to 5 feet
  • Social distance: 5 feet to 12 feet
  • Public distance: 12 feet to 25 feet

    • 3. Chronemics:

  • Chronemics deal with time, pauses and stops which play a significant role while communicating.

    • 4. Paralanguage:

  • Paralanguage deals with vocal qualities such as volume, tone, rate, pronunciation, pitch and intonation patterns. The quality and volume of the voice determines the effectiveness of the message.

    • 5. Appearance:

  • The appearance of a person during the course of communication reflects personality as well as effectiveness of the overall communication. upon the person's personality well as effectiveness of the overall communication.
